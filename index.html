A health assistant that turns real-life barriers into actionable prevention.

Older adults who immigrate later in life often face language, identity, and system-navigation barriers. This app collects basic health facts and social determinants of health, then uses ML to suggest personalized lifestyle changes that reduce risk and improve long-term outcomes.

The Problem

Late-life immigrants are underserved

Many seniors immigrate later, and face language barriers, fewer social connections, and difficulty navigating primary care. Prevention becomes reactive‚Äîpeople seek care only when symptoms worsen.

Social determinants drive health outcomes

Income, housing, food access, transportation, isolation, and health literacy often matter as much as medical history‚Äî but most tools ignore them.

The Solution

Inputs

  • Age, sex, height/weight, activity level, sleep
  • Chronic conditions (e.g., diabetes, hypertension)
  • Medication count + adherence difficulty
  • Social determinants: language access, income stability, food access, housing safety, social isolation

Outputs

  • Personalized lifestyle changes (movement, nutrition, sleep, stress)
  • Barrier-aware suggestions (low-cost + culturally practical)
  • Simple explanations and next-step checklists
  • ‚ÄúBring this summary to your doctor‚Äù printable view

How it Works

1) User fills a short intake form Basic health + demographic info plus social factors (language access, food security, transportation, loneliness).
2) ML model estimates preventative risk flags The model predicts risk categories (example: cardiometabolic risk, falls risk, adherence barrier).
3) Recommendation engine generates ‚Äúdoable‚Äù actions Actions are practical for older adults and adjusted for constraints (budget, mobility, language).
4) Explainability layer builds trust The app shows the top 3 reasons behind a recommendation in plain language.

These are prototype-level metrics for a binary risk prediction model. In a real deployment, results would be validated clinically and monitored for bias across subgroups.

What these mean

  • F1 balances precision + recall (overall quality)
  • Sensitivity = correctly catching high-risk users
  • Specificity = correctly identifying low-risk users
  • AUROC = how well the model separates risk vs non-risk
